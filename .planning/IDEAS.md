# Homeowner Page Ideas

Ideas brainstormed during project initialization. Revisit these if the primary direction needs adjustment.

## Selected Direction

**Story of Your Home** — A scrolling narrative timeline that tells the story of a homeowner's journey using the SB7 framework. The homeowner is the hero. Data-backed beats unfold chronologically from purchase to today. Designed to be shareable, personal, and impossible to get from Zillow or Homebot.

## Other Ideas (Parked)

### 1. Home Wealth Dashboard
Frame the home as an *investment*. Show equity earned, cost-per-day of ownership vs renting, home performance vs S&P 500 over the same period. Conversation starter: "Did you know your house outperformed the stock market?"

### 2. "Your Neighborhood Right Now" Intel Brief
Hyper-local snapshot — what sold nearby, who's listing, days on market trends, buyer demand signals. Like a private intelligence report for their street. Shareable because people are nosy about their neighbors.

### 3. Arizona Homeowner Seasonal Guide
Personalized to their home's age, location, and features. "Your AC unit is 8 years old — here's when to expect replacement." "Monsoon season prep for homes with flat roofs in Scottsdale." Practical value that positions Live AZ Co as the team that cares beyond the transaction.

### 4. "What If" Scenario Page
Interactive — "What if you sold today?" "What if you rented it as an STR?" "What if you pulled equity for a second property?" Leverages existing STR calculator and CMA engine. Gets people *thinking* about their next move.

### 5. Home Anniversary Card (Digital)
Simple, beautiful, emotional. "Happy 2nd homeiversary!" Light on data, heavy on brand warmth. Celebratory rather than analytical.

## Key Insight

Several of these ideas could become **sections or modules** within the primary "Story of Your Home" page rather than standalone products. The timeline narrative is the container; these are potential content blocks within it.

## Production Requirements

- Volume: 20-30 pages per month (~200+ total over time)
- Input: Must be fast — address, purchase price, purchase date, client name
- Data: Auto-populated from ARMLS / MLS data where possible
- Differentiation: Must NOT feel like Homebot, Zillow, or any automated home value report
- Brand: Live AZ Co design language, SB7 narrative framework
